## Open items / contract notes

- **Lazy matplotlib helpers** on `Comparison`, `SegmentedPerformanceReport`,
  and the sub-reports are documented in DESIGN.md §3.3 but only
  `TemporalPerformanceReport.plot()` is implemented (P5.A). Phase 6's
  `_repr_html_` polish pass is the natural home for the rest.
- `Comparison.__repr__` with 3+ solutions shows one Δ column (vs the
  first solution) and points the reader at `.delong_pvalues` for the
  full pairwise table. If a richer N-column layout is wanted later
  it's a `__repr__`-only change.
- `compare()` with non-uniform `weights=` returns an all-NaN
  `delong_pvalues` (P3.D's `delong_test` raises `NotImplementedError`
  for weighted variants; the fallback is documented in the
  `Comparison` repr and `notes/P5.C.md`). Weighted DeLong (Pepe 2004
  §5.2) is the right v0+ extension.

## Phase 5 also unblocks

- Deployment-monitoring view: `performance_over_time` lets modelers see
  AUC/KS/Brier/PSI drift over rolling validation windows.
- Subgroup analysis: `performance_by_segment` compares per-segment
  performance against the full-data aggregate.
- Challenger comparisons: `compare({"a": sol_a, "b": sol_b}, data)`
  produces the side-by-side metric table + DeLong p-values DESIGN.md
  §3.3 specifies.
